Motors

When current goes through a wire, a magnetic field is generated around it. The higher the current, the greater the field.

How much torque, rotational force, depends on the motor's input current. More current implies stronger magnetic field in the coils, that means more attraction/repulsion.

How much current a motor can tolerate depends on how thick the coils are (thicker implies more current and hence more torque).
